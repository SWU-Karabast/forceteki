# 3. Card Types

> Source: Star Wars Unlimited Comprehensive Rules v7.0 (3/6/26)

---

## 3.1 General

- 3.1.1 Six card types: **base, event, leader, unit, upgrade, token.** Each card can be one or more types.
- 3.1.2 "Resource" is NOT a card type; it is a game object a card becomes when placed in the resource zone. See 1.7.

---

## 3.2 Base

- 3.2.1 A base represents a location. Each base has a name, trait(s), HP value, and aspect icon. Some have abilities.
  - a. Some bases have abilities affecting deck construction that override typical limitations.
- 3.2.2 Each deck must have exactly 1 base. Each base begins in its owner's base zone.
- 3.2.3 Bases can be dealt damage through abilities and enemy attacks. Any unit can attack an enemy base directly unless prevented. When a unit attacks a base, it remains in its arena.
- 3.2.4 Some abilities heal damage from a base (e.g., Restore keyword). A base cannot be healed beyond its HP.
- 3.2.5 When a base is defeated (damage ≥ HP), its owner immediately loses the game. A player cannot resolve actions or abilities/effects once their base reaches 0 remaining HP.

---

## 3.3 Event

- 3.3.1 An event has a name, cost, trait(s), and ability. May also have aspect icons.
- 3.3.2 To play an event: pay its cost (plus any additional costs/restrictions), place it in its owner's discard pile, then resolve its ability. See 6.2.
- 3.3.3 Events never "enter play" — they move directly from hand to discard pile. (A player may place the event in front of them while resolving the ability, but it is in the discard pile for rules purposes.)
- 3.3.4 An event that affects cards in a player's discard pile can affect itself.
- 3.3.5 An event ability must be resolved as completely as possible. A player may play an event even if none of its ability can resolve.
- 3.3.6 If a triggered ability triggers "When you play an event," the event ability must fully resolve before the triggered ability resolves.
- 3.3.7 If an event is played from a discard pile, it re-enters that discard pile as a new copy once played.

---

## 3.4 Leader

- 3.4.1 A leader is a double-sided card: one or more horizontal "Leader" sides, and (usually) one vertical "Leader Unit" side. Each side has two aspect icons, a name, subtitle, trait(s), and abilities. A leader provides its aspect icons to the deck.
- 3.4.2 Each deck must have exactly 1 leader. It begins in the base zone with its Leader side faceup.
- 3.4.3 Only abilities on the faceup side of a leader are "in play" and can be used at a given time.
- 3.4.4 **Deploying a Leader**: Most leaders have an Epic Action or Action ability on their Leader side to deploy.
  - A leader may be deployed ready or exhausted.
  - When deployed as a unit: flip to Leader Unit side, move to ground arena, ready. It is "deployed," not "played." Leader side leaves play; Leader Unit side enters play simultaneously. It can attack, be attacked, and use Leader Unit abilities.
  - a. Some leaders deploy as upgrades: flip to Leader Unit side and attach to an eligible unit in either arena. Simultaneously, Leader side leaves play and Leader Upgrade enters play.
  - b. If a leader flips via a non-deploy ability, it is not considered deployed or played, but it is still a Leader Unit/Upgrade while in play.
- 3.4.5 When a Leader Unit or Leader Upgrade is defeated: flip to Leader side, move to base zone exhausted. Leader Unit side leaves play; Leader side enters play simultaneously. If Epic Action was previously used to deploy, place an epic action counter on it (can't be used again). Other Leader side abilities can still be used.
- 3.4.6 If an ability would cause a Leader Unit to move to an out-of-play zone or change control, it is defeated instead.
- 3.4.7 Some upgrades give the attached unit the "Leader Unit" designation. That unit is treated as a Leader Unit for all "leader"/"non-leader" ability references, but does not provide aspect icons. When defeated, it goes to the discard pile.

---

## 3.5 Unit

- 3.5.1 A unit depicts a Star Wars character or vehicle. Each unit has a name, cost, power, HP, trait(s), and arena type. May also have subtitle, aspect icons, abilities, and a unique icon.
- 3.5.2 To play a unit: pay its cost (plus any additional costs/restrictions), place it in its designated arena. See 6.2.
- 3.5.3 A unit enters play exhausted and remains until defeated.
- 3.5.4 A unit is defeated when damage ≥ HP or an ability defeats it. Goes to owner's discard pile.
- 3.5.5 A Leader Unit is a type of unit. Same rules as non-leader units, except it's deployed rather than played, and returns to the base zone instead of being discarded.
- 3.5.6 Some units can attach to units as upgrades (via ability). When attached as an upgrade: all damage is removed, all upgrades on that unit are defeated. While attached, it is an upgrade, not a unit.

---

## 3.6 Upgrade

- 3.6.1 An upgrade attaches to a unit. Has name, cost, trait(s), power modifier, and HP modifier. May also have aspect icons, abilities, and a unique icon.
- 3.6.2 To play an upgrade: pay its cost, attach it to a unit in play (tuck it halfway under the unit, showing ability and modifiers). See 6.2.
- 3.6.3 All upgrades have attachment restrictions. Default: "Attach to a unit." If no eligible unit is in play, the upgrade can't be played.
  - a. A unit is "eligible" only when the upgrade is played or attached; eligibility isn't rechecked after.
  - b. While a non-upgrade card is attached as an upgrade, it has the attachment restriction from the original ability.
  - c. Some unit abilities may override an upgrade's attachment restriction.
- 3.6.4 No limit to the number of upgrades attached to a unit. An upgrade can only attach to one unit at a time.
- 3.6.5 Upgrades may be played on friendly or enemy units. The player who plays the upgrade remains its controller.
- 3.6.6 If an upgrade detaches from a unit, the unit also detaches from that upgrade (and vice versa).
- 3.6.7 Each upgrade has a power modifier and HP modifier, applied cumulatively to the attached unit.
- 3.6.8 If an upgrade says "attached unit gains X," that text is an ability the unit has while the upgrade is attached; it can be lost, ignored, or affected by other abilities.
- 3.6.9 If a unit has multiple upgrades giving the same ability, it gains that ability multiple times. Keywords don't stack unless followed by a numeral.
- 3.6.10 Some upgrade abilities affect the attached unit but don't say "attached unit gains" — those abilities still apply even if the unit "loses all abilities."
- 3.6.11 An upgrade is defeated when its attached unit leaves play, or when an ability defeats it directly. When defeated, the unit loses the power, HP, and abilities the upgrade gave it; the upgrade goes to owner's discard pile.
- 3.6.12 Token upgrades follow upgrade rules, but are set aside when defeated.
- 3.6.13 A unit is "upgraded" while it has at least one upgrade attached.
- 3.6.14 An upgrade is always attached to a unit while in play. If attached to a new unit mid-play, it detaches from the first and attaches to the new one simultaneously.

---

## 3.7 Token

- 3.7.1 A token doesn't start in a player's deck. If it has a second type (e.g., "token upgrade," "token unit"), it follows the rules of that type. If not, it has a subtype indicating special rules.
- 3.7.2 Tokens are set aside at the start of the game. They are "created" not "played" (no "When Played" triggers), but are considered to enter play when created. Tokens have a cost of 0.
  - a. Keywords on token units apply as normal, but "When Played" style triggers fire like "When Created" instead.
- 3.7.3 Tokens can't enter out-of-play zones (hand, discard, deck). When they leave play, set them aside. They are still considered to have left play.
- 3.7.4 No limit on available tokens. A player may substitute any object for a token.

### Specific Token Types

- **3.7.5 Experience token**: Token upgrade. LEARNED trait. Gives attached unit +1 power and +1 HP. Multiple Experience tokens each add their bonus cumulatively.
- **3.7.6 Shield token**: Token upgrade. ARMOR trait. +0/+0. Text: "If damage would be dealt to attached unit, prevent that damage. If you do, defeat a Shield token on it." Only one Shield token is defeated per instance of damage even if multiple are attached.
- **3.7.7 Battle Droid token**: Token unit. Ground. SEPARATIST, DROID, TROOPER traits. Villainy aspect. 1 power / 1 HP.
- **3.7.8 Clone Trooper token**: Token unit. Ground. REPUBLIC, CLONE, TROOPER traits. Heroism aspect. 2 power / 2 HP.
- **3.7.9 TIE Fighter token**: Token unit. Space. VEHICLE, FIGHTER traits. Villainy aspect. 1 power / 1 HP.
- **3.7.10 X-Wing token**: Token unit. Space. VEHICLE, FIGHTER traits. Heroism aspect. 2 power / 2 HP.
- **3.7.11 Force token**: Token. Name "The Force." Subtype "Force Token." No traits. Created in a player's base zone. A player can't control more than one Force token. See 8.38.
- **3.7.12 Spy token**: Token unit. Ground. OFFICIAL trait. No aspects. 0 power / 2 HP. Has Raid 2.
- **3.7.13 Credit token**: Token. Name "Credit." Subtype "Credit Token." SUPPLY trait. Text: "While paying resources, you may defeat this token. If you do, pay 1 less." Created in a player's resource zone, but are NOT resources.
