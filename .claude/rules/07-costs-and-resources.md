# Costs and Resources

> Source: Star Wars Unlimited Comprehensive Rules v7.0 (3/6/26)
> See also: 01-overview.md sections 1.7 and 1.8 for base rules; 04-combat.md section 6.2 for "Play a Card" action steps.

---

## Resources (1.7 recap)

- A resource is a game object (not a card type) used to pay costs. A card becomes a resource when placed in a player's resource zone.
- Resources are placed facedown and remain facedown. A player may view their own at any time.
- The number of ready/exhausted resources is public; their identity is hidden from the opponent.
- To pay a resource cost: exhaust ready resources equal to the cost. An exhausted resource cannot pay a cost.
- Players may resource one card from their hand during each regroup phase.
- When resourced, a card is facedown and exhausted. Its printed attributes are inactive while it's a resource (exception: Smuggle is active while a card is facedown as a resource).
- Credit tokens are created in the resource zone but are NOT resources.

---

## Cost Rules (1.8 recap)

- A card's cost = the number in the yellow box (top left). This many resources must be exhausted to play it.
- **Modified cost** = printed cost + increases (including aspect penalty) − decreases.
- Cost cannot be modified below 0.
- "For free" = bypass ALL resource modifiers including aspect penalty (additional non-resource costs still apply).
- When abilities refer to a card's "cost," they always mean the **printed cost**, not the modified cost.
- Modifiers to cost are only active while the card is being played (not at other times).

## Aspect Penalty (8.1)

- If a player plays a card with aspect icons not provided by their leader and/or base, they pay +2 resources per unmatched icon.
- Each leader/base icon provides only 1 icon of that type. To avoid a penalty on a card with 2 of the same icon, the deck's leader and base must together provide 2 of that type.
- "For free" bypasses the aspect penalty entirely.
- Ignoring the aspect penalty (via abilities like Hera Syndulla) does not remove the icons from the card — other abilities that depend on aspects still apply.

---

## Modifiers (8.16)

- A "modifier" changes a printed value on a card through an ability or an attached upgrade, creating a "modified value."
- When calculating a modified value: start with printed value, apply increases first, then decreases.
- Modifiers are cumulative. When a new modifier is applied, recalculate immediately.
- A value cannot be modified below 0 — treat as 0. Subsequent modifiers still account for earlier ones even while the value is at 0.
- **Modifying Cost** (8.16.5):
  - Cost modifiers only apply while the card is being played.
  - If an ability checks a card's "cost," it checks the printed cost only.
  - "For free" bypasses all resource costs including aspect penalties.
- **Modifying Power** (8.16.6):
  - Modifiers only apply while the card is in play. When a card leaves play, it loses modifiers. For "When Defeated" abilities, use the last known power value.
  - Each attached upgrade cumulatively modifies power.
- **Modifying HP** (8.16.7):
  - When determining remaining HP: subtract damage from modified HP.
  - Modifiers only apply while the card is in play.
  - If removal of an upgrade or expiration of an effect causes remaining HP to reach 0 or less, immediately defeat that unit.

## Additional Costs

- Some abilities add an "additional cost" (non-resource) to play a card. Both the resource cost and all additional costs must be paid to play the card.
- If a replacement effect replaces part/all of a cost, the cost is considered paid as long as the replacement effect can be resolved. If the replacement can't be resolved, the original cost must be paid in full.

## Exploit X (Quick Reference)

See 05-keywords.md for the full definition. In brief:
- While playing a card with Exploit X, you may defeat up to X friendly units. Each defeated unit reduces the card's cost by 2.
- Exploit reduces cost during Step 3 (Determine Costs) of the Play a Card action.
- "When Defeated" abilities from Exploit-defeated units resolve after the Play a Card action, alongside the played card's "When Played" abilities.

---

## Smuggle [Y] (Quick Reference)

See 05-keywords.md for the full definition. In brief:
- Lets a player play the card from their resource zone by paying cost Y instead of its printed cost.
- The card is replaced in the resource zone by the top card of the deck (both enter play simultaneously).
- Active while the card is facedown as a resource.
- Can exhaust itself to help pay its own Smuggle cost.
- Playing via Smuggle is a modified "Play a Card" action. "When Played" abilities trigger.

---

## Plot (Quick Reference)

See 05-keywords.md for the full definition. In brief:
- Active while the card is facedown as a resource.
- When a leader is deployed, you may play Plot cards from your resource zone at the printed cost.
- The card is replaced by the top card of the deck.
