# 1. Game Concepts & 2. Card Anatomy

> Source: Star Wars Unlimited Comprehensive Rules v7.0 (3/6/26)

---

## 1.1 General

- 1.1.1 These Comprehensive Rules apply to any game of Star Wars™: Unlimited, unless otherwise specified.
- 1.1.2 To play, players need one deck each, an initiative counter, damage counters, and token cards matching any types created by their decks.

---

## 1.2 Deck

- 1.2.1 No maximum deck size. Minimum varies by format.
- 1.2.2 Leaders and bases are part of a deck for deckbuilding but not during gameplay.
- 1.2.3 Tokens are not part of a player's deck and don't count toward deck size.
- 1.2.4 Players may include cards from any aspect; playing cards with unprovided aspects incurs the aspect penalty. See 8.1.

---

## 1.3 Golden Rules

- 1.3.1 **Card and Rulebook Precedence**: If this document contradicts the Quickstart, this document takes precedence. If a card contradicts this document, the card takes precedence.
- 1.3.2 **Do as Much as You Can**: While resolving an ability, resolve as much as possible and ignore parts that can't be resolved.
- 1.3.3 **Restrictions Override Permissions**: Restrictive abilities take precedence over permissive abilities.

---

## 1.4 Players, Active Player

- 1.4.1 A player is a person participating in the game.
- 1.4.2 A player's opponent is the other person playing the game.
- 1.4.3 The active player is the player currently taking an action.
- 1.4.4 The first active player starts with the initiative counter. Players alternate taking actions until each player has passed consecutively.
- 1.4.5 If no player is currently taking an action (e.g., during the regroup phase), the player with the initiative counter is the active player.

---

## 1.5 Cards

### 1.5.1 General

- a. Six card types: bases, events, leaders, units, upgrades, and tokens.
- b. A card placed in a resource zone becomes a "resource" (not a card type). See 1.7.

### 1.5.2 Ownership and Control

- a. A player "owns" cards that started the game in their deck (including their leader, base, and any non-upgrade tokens they create).
- b. A player "controls" a card they put into play. They keep control until the card leaves play or the opponent takes control.
- c. While controlling a card, a player may resolve action abilities on it, attack with it if it's a unit, and affect it with "friendly" abilities. They must also resolve any triggered abilities on it.
- d. "Take control": the new controller keeps the card's ready/exhausted status, damage counters, and attached upgrades. The card is oriented to face its new controller.
- e. A player may play an upgrade onto an enemy unit. That player remains the upgrade's controller.
- f. A player owns and controls token upgrades attached to units they control.

### 1.5.3 Friendly and Enemy

- a. A card a player controls is "friendly" for that player.
- b. A card an opponent controls is "enemy" for that player.

### 1.5.4 Ready and Exhausted

- a. A card is "ready" when upright; "exhausted" when turned sideways (rotated 90°).
- b. Each non-leader unit and resource enters play exhausted. Upgrades are neither ready nor exhausted.
- c. Exhausted cards ready via certain abilities or during the regroup phase. See 5.5.
- d. A ready unit must exhaust when starting an attack or paying a cost requiring exhaustion. An exhausted unit cannot attack or pay exhaustion costs.
- e. A ready card chosen for a readying effect doesn't change orientation and isn't considered "readied" for "If you do" effects; similarly for exhausted cards.
- f. When paying a cost using the exhaust icon, the player must exhaust ready resources equal to the cost number.

### 1.5.5 Defeating Cards

- a. In-play cards (bases, tokens, units, upgrades, resources) can be defeated.
- b. A base is defeated when its damage ≥ HP. Its owner loses the game.
- c. A non-leader unit is defeated when its damage ≥ HP or an ability defeats it directly. It goes to its owner's discard pile.
- d. An upgrade is defeated when the unit it's attached to leaves play, or an ability defeats it directly. Goes to owner's discard pile.
- e. A token is defeated like its non-token card type, then set aside out-of-play.
- f. A resource is defeated when an ability defeats it. Goes to owner's discard pile faceup. Does NOT trigger "When Defeated" abilities.

### 1.5.6 Aspects

- a. Aspects are colored icons representing philosophies. The six aspects are: **Vigilance**, **Command**, **Aggression**, **Cunning**, **Villainy**, and **Heroism**.
- b. A deck's leader and base provide their aspect icons. Playing a card with icons beyond those provided incurs the aspect penalty: +2 resources per extra icon. See 8.1.
- c. Neutral cards (no aspect icons, depicted with a gray border) don't incur aspect penalties.

---

## 1.6 Abilities

- 1.6.1 An ability is special text on a card that explains how it affects the game. Multiple abilities each begin on a new line.
- 1.6.2 When resolving an ability, resolve as much as possible and ignore anything that can't resolve.
- 1.6.3 An ability that can be resolved must resolve, unless it includes "you may."
- 1.6.4 Five ability types: action abilities, constant abilities, event abilities, keyword abilities, and triggered abilities. See Section 7.

---

## 1.7 Resources

- 1.7.1 A resource is a game object used to pay costs. A card becomes a resource when placed in a player's resource zone.
- 1.7.2 To pay a cost using the exhaust icon, a player must exhaust ready resources equal to the cost. An exhausted resource can't pay a cost.
- 1.7.3 Resources are placed facedown and remain facedown. A player may view their own facedown resources at any time.
- 1.7.4 The number of ready and exhausted resources is public information. A player may rearrange resources at any time up to the point of a specific resource being chosen, but must announce the intent to rearrange first.
- 1.7.5 If a player controls resources owned by an opponent, the ownership is open information.
- 1.7.6 Players may add a card from their hand to their resource zone during each regroup phase. See 5.5.
- 1.7.7 When an ability instructs a player to "resource a card," it's placed facedown and exhausted in their resource zone. It's not "played" and no "When Played" abilities trigger.
- 1.7.8 While a card is a resource, it's a blank resource card. Its facedown side is out-of-play. Its printed attributes (name, aspects, traits, abilities) are inactive. *(Exception: Smuggle — that ability remains active.)*

---

## 1.8 Cost

- 1.8.1 A card's cost is the number indicated in the yellow box (top left). That many resources must be exhausted to play it.
- 1.8.2 Cost can be modified by abilities and the aspect penalty. If a player can't pay the modified cost, they can't play the card.
- 1.8.3 Cost modifiers are cumulative. Modifiers that increase cost are applied before modifiers that decrease it.
- 1.8.4 A card's cost cannot be modified below 0.
- 1.8.5 If a card is played "for free," all resource modifiers (including aspect penalty) are bypassed. Additional non-resource costs still apply.
- 1.8.6 When an ability refers to the "cost" of a card, it always refers to the **printed cost**, not the modified cost.
- 1.8.7 A Leader Unit's printed cost is its "cost" even if no resources were spent to deploy it.
- 1.8.8 "Additional costs" (non-resource) must also be paid in order to play a card.
- 1.8.9 Action abilities with costs show the cost in brackets after "Action."
- 1.8.10 If a replacement effect replaces part/all of a cost with another effect, the cost is considered paid as long as the replacement can be resolved.

---

## 1.9 Damage

- 1.9.1 Cards with HP (units and bases) can be dealt damage via attacks and abilities. Damage is tracked with damage counters.
- 1.9.2 When damage is dealt to a card, place that many damage counters on it.
- 1.9.3 When damage is healed, remove that many damage counters. Can't heal more counters than are present.
- 1.9.4 A card is "damaged" if it has at least 1 damage counter.
- 1.9.5 Damage is persistent until the card leaves play or damage is healed. When a card leaves play, remove all damage counters.
- 1.9.6 If a unit's damage ≥ HP, it's immediately defeated.
- 1.9.7 If a base's damage ≥ HP, its owner immediately loses the game.
- 1.9.8 If an ability deals damage to multiple units simultaneously; if to one unit in multiple instances, each instance is sequential. Resolve triggered abilities after all damage is dealt.
- 1.9.9 If an ability prevents damage, that damage is not dealt and no abilities that would have triggered if it was dealt will trigger.
- 1.9.10 "Combat damage" is damage dealt during the "End attack" step of an attack (attacker deals to defender/base and defending unit deals to attacker).
- 1.9.11 "Excess damage" is damage that would be dealt to a unit beyond what's needed to defeat it. Overwhelm can affect excess damage.
- 1.9.12 If a unit's ability deals damage, that unit is considered to have dealt that damage.
- 1.9.13 "Unpreventable" damage must be dealt; ignore any prevent abilities.
- 1.9.14 Indirect damage: unpreventable damage assigned by the affected player to their own units and/or base. See 8.36.

---

## 1.10 Power

- 1.10.1 Power = amount of damage a unit deals during combat.
- 1.10.2 Power is shown in the red box (bottom left). May be referred to as "printed power." If changed by an ability, the new value is treated as the printed power.
- 1.10.3 Power can be modified by upgrades or abilities. Modifiers are cumulative. Increases are applied before decreases.
- 1.10.4 Power cannot be modified below 0.
- 1.10.5 If an ability refers to power of a card in play, include modifiers. If out of play, use printed power.
- 1.10.6 If multiple units are tied for most/least power, any one of those units is a valid choice.

---

## 1.11 HP

- 1.11.1 HP (Hit Points) = number of damage counters a card can have before defeat.
- 1.11.2 Units and bases have HP values (units: bottom right; bases: top left). If changed by an ability, the new value is treated as printed HP.
- 1.11.3 HP can be modified by upgrades or abilities. Modifiers are cumulative. Increases before decreases.
- 1.11.4 HP cannot be modified below 0.
- 1.11.5 A card cannot be healed beyond its HP.
- 1.11.6 "Remaining HP" = HP − damage counters (accounting for modifiers). If ≤ 0, card is immediately defeated.
- 1.11.7 If multiple units are tied for most/least remaining HP, any one is a valid choice.

---

## 1.12 Counters

- 1.12.1 Three types: initiative counter, epic action counters, damage counters.
- 1.12.2 **Initiative counter**: Tracks which player has initiative. The player who starts a round with it is the first active player.
  - a. A player can "Take the Initiative" as their action, flipping the counter to its "taken" side. Only once per round; that player must pass for the rest of the action phase.
  - b. At the start of a new round, the initiative counter is flipped back to "available."
  - c. Abilities referencing "if/while you have the initiative" only check if the player controls the counter, not whether it's taken or available.
- 1.12.3 **Epic action counter**: Placed on an Epic Action ability after it's used to show it can't be used again that game.
- 1.12.4 **Damage counters**: Track damage on units and bases. If a unit/base ever has damage ≥ HP, it's defeated.

---

## 1.13 Drawing a Card

- 1.13.1 To draw a card: take the top card of the deck and add it to hand.
- 1.13.2 Multiple cards drawn simultaneously unless the effect specifies "one at a time."
- 1.13.3 If a card's ability triggers when drawn, show it to all opponents before adding to hand. This is not "revealing."

---

## 1.14 Discarding a Card

- 1.14.1 To "discard" = move to owner's discard pile from another zone (usually hand or deck).
- 1.14.2 When an ability discards from a player's hand, the discarding player chooses which to discard, unless otherwise specified.
- 1.14.3 Cards discarded from a deck are always discarded from the top.
- 1.14.4 Multiple discards at once are simultaneous unless stated "one at a time."
- 1.14.5 If a player has no cards in hand, they cannot discard from hand.
- 1.14.6 "Discard your hand" = discard until hand is empty. Already having no cards counts as having discarded.

---

## 1.15 Actions

Five actions available during the action phase:

1. **Play a Card** — Pay resources equal to cost (with modifiers), then put the card into play (unit/upgrade) or resolve its ability (event). See 6.2.
2. **Attack With a Unit** — Exhaust a ready unit and choose an enemy unit in the same arena or the opponent's base to attack. See 6.3.
3. **Use an Action Ability** — Choose a card with an action ability, pay its cost, resolve its effect. See 6.4.
4. **Take the Initiative** — Take the initiative counter, flip to "taken" side. Only one player per round. After taking it, that player passes for the rest of the phase.
   - a. Only one player can take initiative per round.
   - b. After taking initiative, the player passes for all remaining turns that round, but still resolves triggered abilities.
   - c. If initiative is taken immediately after the opponent passes, the action phase ends.
   - d. If no player takes initiative, the player with it keeps it and starts the next round as first active player.
5. **Pass** — Declare passing. The player has done nothing. May pass even if other actions are available.
   - When both players pass consecutively (including when one takes initiative after the other passes), the action phase ends.

---

## 1.16 Game State

- 1.16.1 "The game state" refers to: each card's current zone, controller, attributes, and status; the initiative counter's controller and status; the status of open/hidden information; active lasting and delayed effects; Epic Action statuses.
- 1.16.2 A player must change the game state when taking any action other than passing. If an action wouldn't change the game state, choose a different action or pass.
- 1.16.3 Examples of changing game state: paying a cost, drawing/discarding, readying/exhausting, damaging/healing, looking at/searching/revealing, moving zones, modifying power/HP, creating effects, taking initiative, triggering Epic Actions, using use-restricted abilities.
- 1.16.4 A game state change must be caused by a rule or ability; a player cannot make arbitrary changes.
- 1.16.5 Certain situations require immediate resolution (in priority order):
  - a. A player's base is defeated → that player loses.
  - b. A player controls multiple copies of a unique card → defeat copies until only one remains.
  - c. An upgrade is no longer attached to a unit → that upgrade is defeated.
  - d. A unit guarding a captured unit is no longer in play → the captured unit enters play under its owner's control, exhausted.
  - e. A unit has 0 remaining HP → it is defeated.

---

## 1.17 Open and Hidden Information

- 1.17.1 **Open information** includes: faceup card attributes in play (including modifiers), counter counts, number of cards in hand/deck/discard, card attributes in discard piles, the reverse side of each player's leader and captured cards. Cannot be withheld from opponents.
- 1.17.2 **Hidden information** = all information not open. Order of cards in deck, contents of hands and facedown resources. May be hidden to one player but not both.
- 1.17.3 Players may only view hidden-information cards when an ability explicitly allows it.
- 1.17.4 A player may resolve an ability involving hidden information as though they have fewer options than they really do, but still must change the game state in some way.

---

## 1.18 Determining Responsibility (for defeating units)

To determine which player is responsible for defeating a unit (in order):

- a. If defeated because of damage dealt by a unit a player controls → that player is responsible.
- b. If defeated by an ability controlled by a player → that player is responsible.
- c. If defeated during the regroup phase or when a lasting effect expires → no player is responsible.
- d. If defeated by 0 remaining HP → the most recent player whose ability/effect changed its remaining HP is responsible.
- e. Otherwise → the active player is responsible.

## 2. Card Anatomy

- 2.1.1 Card parts: name, subtitle, card type, arena type, cost, aspect(s), power, power modifier, HP, HP modifier, trait(s), text box, credit line. All except credit line are "attributes."
- **Name** (2.2): Top of card. Considered English version regardless of printed language. Unique icon (◆) before name indicates a unique card.
- **Subtitle** (2.3): Below name if present. No mechanical function; distinguishes unique cards sharing a name.
- **Card Type** (2.4): Top left. Can be one or more types: base, event, leader, unit, upgrade, token.
- **Arena Type** (2.5): Top right. Ground or space. Only units have an arena type.
- **Cost** (2.6): Top left corner. Resources required to play. For leaders, related to deploying.
- **Aspects** (2.7): Aspect icons on sides of card. Bases and leaders provide aspects; units/events/upgrades incur penalties for unmatched aspects. 0–2 icons per card. Six aspects: Vigilance, Command, Aggression, Cunning, Villainy, Heroism. Cards with no icons are "neutral."
- **Power** (2.8): Left side. Damage dealt in combat. Only units.
- **Power Modifier** (2.9): Left side of upgrades and PILOT units. Modifies attached unit's power.
- **HP** (2.10): Right side (units) or top left (bases). Damage threshold before defeat. "Remaining HP" = HP − damage counters.
- **HP Modifier** (2.11): Right side of upgrades and PILOT units. Modifies attached unit's HP.
- **Traits** (2.12): Below name (events), below art (units), or bottom (upgrades). No inherent rules; referenced by some abilities. Units with the PILOT trait can be in play as a unit or upgrade.
- **Text Box** (2.13): Contains abilities. Italicized parenthetical text is "reminder text" (a simplified guide; doesn't override rules). Split text boxes on PILOTs are visual cues only — all text applies in both modes.
- **Credit Line** (2.14): Bottom of card. Artist, licensing, set/language/rarity/number. No gameplay effect.
