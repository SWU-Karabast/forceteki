# 8. Additional Rules, Formats, and Special Mechanics

> Source: Star Wars Unlimited Comprehensive Rules v7.0 (3/6/26)

---

## 8.2 Attribute

- An attribute is a part of a card's anatomy: name, subtitle, card type, arena type, cost, aspect(s), power, power modifier, HP, HP modifier, trait(s), and text box.

---

## 8.3 "Can" and "Can't"

- **"Can"**: adjusts or overrides a default rule. The player may choose whether to use it or follow the default.
- **"Can't"**: overrides a default rule. The player must follow the "can't" over the default.
- **Restriction overrides permission**: "can't" takes precedence over "may" or "can."
  - *Example: "can attack a base this phase" + "can't attack bases" → can't attack bases.*
  - a. Units with Sentinel can always be attacked even if they also have a "can't be attacked" ability.
  - b. Unpreventable damage is always dealt even if a unit/base has a "can't be damaged" ability.

---

## 8.4 Choose

- When an ability says "choose" a game object, the chosen object must match the specified criteria.
- "Choose a unit" = unit must be in play unless otherwise specified.
- "Choose a player" = either player, including the resolving player.
- "Choose X options from a bulleted list" = choose a different option each time; one at a time; may wait to see how one resolves before choosing the next.
- "Deal damage divided as you choose" = choose how much goes to each eligible card, up to the total. Must deal all damage specified. All damage is simultaneous.
  - a. "A number" or "any number" includes 0.

---

## 8.5 Copy

- A card is a "copy" of another if both have all the same printed attributes (name, subtitle, uniqueness, card type, arena type, cost, aspect(s), trait(s), power/modifier, HP/modifier, and abilities — not reminder text).
- Same name, different other attributes = not copies.
- Printed attributes determine copies; modifiers and effects are ignored.
- Whenever a card leaves and re-enters play, it's a "new copy" — does not retain modifiers or prior effects.
- A player may have up to 3 copies of any non-leader, non-base card in their deck.
- A player can only control 1 copy of each unique card at a time. See 8.30.
- A player may control any number of non-unique card copies.

---

## 8.6 Empty Deck

- If a player would draw from an empty deck: deal 3 damage to their base for each card they would draw (instead of drawing).
  - *Example: Drawing 2 cards from an empty deck = 6 damage to base.*
- If an ability would cause a player to shuffle, discard, search, reveal, or look at cards from an empty deck: ignore that part.

---

## 8.7 Enters Play

- A card enters play when it moves from an out-of-play zone to an in-play zone, or when turned faceup.
- When a leader is deployed, its Leader Unit side enters play. When a Leader Unit is defeated, its Leader side enters play.
- Whenever a card enters play, it's a "new copy." This applies to cards played from the discard pile and cards that re-enter play after being rescued.

---

## 8.8 First, Second, Etc.

- "First" occurrence in a phase/round = the very first that phase/round, not the first after an ability becomes active. Does not apply retroactively.
- "Second" or later = likewise refers to that specific occurrence in the phase/round. Also does not apply retroactively.

---

## 8.9 "If You Do"

- "If you do" makes part of an ability conditional on another part resolving. If the condition is fulfilled, the player must resolve the conditional text.
- If a replacement effect replaces the text before "If you do," the controlling player is still considered to have resolved it and still resolves the "If you do" text.

---

## 8.10 Ignore

- "Ignore" a keyword ability = treat that ability as inactive.
- "Ignore the aspect penalty" = pay without adding the aspect penalty. The card still has those aspect icons (other abilities that check aspects still apply). Other additional costs still apply.

---

## 8.11 Last Known Information

- "Last Known Information" = a snapshot of a card's status immediately before it left play. Includes: attributes, controller, modifiers, ready/exhausted status, attached upgrades, and how it was defeated/removed. Used primarily when resolving "When Defeated" abilities.
- Last Known Information is reference only. Other abilities on a card that left play are not active while resolving its "When Defeated" abilities.

---

## 8.12 Leaves Play

- A card leaves play when it moves from an in-play zone to an out-of-play zone, or when turned facedown. When a unit leaves play, any attached upgrades are defeated, but lasting/delayed effects from its abilities remain active.
- When a leader is deployed, its Leader side leaves play. When a Leader Unit is defeated, its Leader Unit side leaves play.

---

## 8.13 Look At

- "Look at" cards from a zone = pick them up and view them, keeping secret from other players. Cannot change their order. Return in the same orientation unless otherwise specified.
- Looked-at cards are not considered to leave their zone unless otherwise specified.

---

## 8.14 Lose, Loses

- "Lose" a keyword: unit ceases to have it. Abilities granting it also cease for the duration. Cannot regain that keyword during the effect's duration.
- "Lose all abilities": the card ceases to have ANY abilities (including those given by other cards) for the duration. Cannot gain abilities during the effect's duration.

---

## 8.16 Modifiers (see 07-costs-and-resources.md for full details)

A "modifier" changes a printed value. Modified value = printed + increases − decreases. Cannot go below 0.

---

## 8.17 "Must"

- "Must" = the player has to resolve as specified.
- "Must" takes precedence over "can."
- "Can't" takes precedence over "must."

---

## 8.18 "Name"

- "Name a card" = clearly state any card name in Star Wars: Unlimited. Doesn't account for subtitles or unique icon.
- A card "shares a name" if their full names are identical (subtitle not required to match).

---

## 8.19 Other, Another, Different

- "Other" / "another" = cannot apply to itself.
- May be used to separate the first card affected from subsequent cards.

---

## 8.20 Play Restrictions

- A play restriction prevents a card from being played. Checked before paying costs.
- "Attach to" is a play restriction for upgrades. If no eligible unit is in play, the upgrade can't be played.
- "Can't play" is a play restriction in certain card abilities.

---

## 8.21 Prevent (Damage)

- If all damage is prevented: no counters placed; no "damage dealt" abilities trigger.
- If specific amount is prevented: reduce the damage placed by that amount; if all prevented, no "damage dealt" abilities trigger.

---

## 8.22 Printed

- "Printed" = an attribute physically printed on the card, as opposed to modified values. Used to differentiate from modified values.

---

## 8.23 Random

- When choosing randomly, any method with equal likelihood for all options is acceptable (e.g., flipping a coin).

---

## 8.24 Referential Abilities

- A referential ability refers to a specific card by name.
- Only accounts for the card's name, not subtitle or card type.
- Some upgrade referential abilities specify an effect if the attached unit is the named unit; the upgrade can still attach to other units, but the referenced ability won't resolve.

---

## 8.25 Return

- "Return" a card = move it to its owner's hand. By default, only applies to in-play cards unless otherwise specified.
- A card returned from play to hand is NOT considered defeated.

---

## 8.26 Reveal

- "Reveal" = temporarily make a card open information by showing it to all players.
- When revealing from hand, the player chooses which cards to reveal (matching ability criteria).
- Revealed cards are not considered to have left their zone.
- After resolving, return revealed cards to their zone in the same orientation as before.
- If a resource is revealed, show the faceup side (the side with attributes).

---

## 8.27 Search

- "Search" a deck = look through a specified number of cards, find one or more, return the rest as directed by the ability.
- If searching the top X cards: put unchosen cards on the bottom in random order. If searching the entire deck: shuffle after.
- A player may resolve as though no appropriate card was found.
- Cards searched in a deck are not considered to leave the deck while searching. Chosen cards are in the deck zone (but no longer in the deck) until moved by drawing, discarding, or playing.
- If playing a card after finding it in a search, restrictions from the search also apply to the Play a Card action. Unplayable cards must be returned to the bottom of the deck.

---

## 8.28 Take Control

- "Take control" = the player becomes the card's controller until it leaves play or an opponent takes control.
  - a. A unit maintains ready/exhausted status, damage counters, and attached upgrades (with original controllers). New controller orients it to face them.
  - b. An upgrade remains on the unit it's attached to unless otherwise specified.
  - c. A resource maintains its ready/exhausted status and moves to the new controller's resource zone.
- When a unit is defeated, it goes to its **owner's** discard pile; "When Defeated" abilities are resolved by whoever controlled it.
- If control of an attacking/defending unit changes during an attack, that unit is no longer part of the attack.

---

## 8.29 "Then"

- "Then" separates two sequential effects. Resolve the first completely, then the second. If the first can't resolve, the second still resolves.
- Abilities triggered by either effect are not resolved until both effects finish resolving.

---

## 8.30 Unique, Unique Icon (◆)

- A unique card is indicated with ◆ before the name. Each unique card has a subtitle.
- A player can only control one copy of each unique card with the same **name and subtitle** at a time. If a player ever has more than one in play, defeat one immediately (not a triggered ability; still resolve any abilities from playing/defeating).
  - *Example: Luke Skywalker: Faithful Friend and Luke Skywalker: Jedi Knight have different subtitles, so both can be controlled simultaneously.*
  - a. The unique copy limit applies to all in-play copies, regardless of how they're in play (e.g., as a unit AND as an upgrade simultaneously).
- The unique limit is player-specific. Each player can control their own copy of the same unique card simultaneously.

---

## 8.31 Up To

- "Up to X" = choose any integer from 0 to X.
- Choosing 0 for defeating, dealing damage, healing, or attacking = not considered to have done that action.

---

## 8.32 "You"

- "You" on a card = its controller.
- In triggered ability conditions, "when you do X" = any cards you control or abilities you resolve.

---

## 8.33 "You May"

- "You may" = the player can choose whether or not to resolve the specified text. If they choose to, they must resolve as much as possible.
- "You may... If you do..." = if the player fully resolves the "you may" part, they must also resolve the "if you do" part.
- "You may... Use this ability only once each round." = if the player resolves it, they can't use it again that round. Only considered "used" if the player chooses to resolve the text.

---

## 8.34 Capture

- "Capture" a unit = place it facedown under the capturing unit. Remove all damage counters; defeat all upgrades on the captured unit. The captured card leaves play and is out-of-play but remains open information.
  - a. If the capturing unit is no longer in play when resolving the capture effect, ignore that part.
- A unit guarding one or more captured units is "guarding" them.
- "Rescue" a captured unit = retrieve it from under its guard unit, flip it faceup, and return it to play exhausted under its owner's control. It enters play but is not "played" (no "When Played" abilities trigger).
- If a guarding unit leaves play: immediately rescue all captured units it was guarding.
- If a token unit would be captured: set it aside (still considered to leave play).

---

## 8.35 "For Each"

- "For each" abilities: determine how each effect will resolve, then resolve all simultaneously.
- If "for each" deals damage, all damage is calculated and dealt as one instance.

---

## 8.36 Indirect Damage

- "Deal X indirect damage to a player" = choose a player; they must assign X unpreventable damage, divided as they choose, among any number of their units and/or base.
- Indirect damage is unpreventable — ignores Shield tokens and "can't be damaged" abilities.
  - a. If assigned to a unit with Shield tokens, damage is placed as though no Shields exist; Shields are not defeated.
- A player cannot assign more indirect damage to a unit than its remaining HP.
- Indirect damage is considered dealt by the player/unit whose ability caused it.
- All indirect damage from a single ability is simultaneous.

---

## 8.37 Move

- "Move" a card to an arena = place it into that arena as a unit. Upgrades, damage, and ready/exhausted status are maintained.
- Moving to an arena is NOT considered entering or leaving play.

---

## 8.38 The Force

- The Force token is created in a player's base zone. A player can only control one Force token at a time. If instructed to create a Force token when they already have one, ignore that part.
- "The Force is with you" = that player creates their Force token.
- "The Force is with you" is true if the player currently controls their Force token.
- "Use the Force" = defeat your Force token. You may not Use the Force if you don't control it.

---

## 8.39 Disclose

- "Disclose" X aspect icons = reveal any number of cards from hand. If the revealed cards collectively have at least the specified icons, the condition is fulfilled.
- A player may reveal more cards than necessary.
- If the requirement can't be met, the player is not considered to have revealed any cards.

---

# 9. Two-Player Constructed Formats

## 9.2 Premier Format
- Deck: exactly 1 leader + exactly 1 base + minimum 50 other cards (units, events, upgrades).
- No more than 3 copies of any unit, event, or upgrade.
- May have a sideboard of up to 10 cards.

## 9.3 Trilogy Format
- Three decks, best-of-three match.
- Each Trilogy deck: exactly 1 leader + 1 base + minimum 50 other cards.
- All deckbuilding restrictions apply to the **combined** three decks: max 1 copy of any leader/base and max 3 copies of any unit/event/upgrade across all three decks combined.
- No sideboards.
- **Ban Phase**: Players present their three leader-base pairings. Each secretly bans one opponent deck. Both reveal simultaneously. Banned decks are unavailable. Players secretly choose and simultaneously reveal their game 1 decks.
- After a game, the winning deck is removed. In a draw, replay with the same decks.

---

# 10. Limited Formats

## 10.2 Sealed Format
- Deck: exactly 1 leader + 1 base + minimum 30 other cards.
- Each player opens 6 booster packs. May use any leader from their packs. May use any base from their packs or borrow any Common-rarity base of the appropriate set.
- No limit on copies of a single card.

## 10.3 Draft Format
- Each player opens 3 booster packs. Draft by choosing one card from a group, passing the rest.
- Leaders are drafted first (and are public knowledge). Tokens and Common bases are set aside before drafting other cards.
- Deck: exactly 1 leader + 1 base + minimum 30 other cards. Must use one of the three drafted leaders.
- No copy limit for drafted cards.

---

# 11. Playing With More Than Two Players

## 11.1 General
- Multiplayer = more than two players (usually 3–4).
- Same rules as two-player except as specified below.

## 11.2 Gameplay Overview
- Each player has their own portion of the ground and space arenas.
- Setup same as two-player: first player decides, then clockwise order for mulligans.
- Players take turns clockwise from the first active player.
- Action phase ends when each player passes consecutively.

## 11.3 Player Elimination
- Once a player's base reaches 0 HP, they are eliminated. All their cards are removed from play. Any captured units from their units are returned to their owners.
- Removed cards from an eliminated player are NOT "defeated" for ability purposes.
- An eliminated player's lasting/delayed effects remain active and must be resolved by remaining players.
- If an eliminated player controlled the initiative, return it to the center "available" side up.

## 11.4 Additional Clarifications
- "Opponent" = any one other player in the game. "Opponents" = all other players.
- Deals between players are not binding.
- Sentinel only prevents units in the same arena from attacking that player's non-Sentinel units or base — not from attacking a different opponent's units/base.
- During an attack, if the defending player loses control of the defending unit to a player other than the attacking player, the attack continues with the new controller as the defending player.

# 12. Twin Suns (Multiplayer Format)

## 12.1 General
- Special multiplayer format. Each player's deck has **two leaders** instead of one. Each deck can only contain one copy of each card.
- All multiplayer rules apply unless otherwise specified.

## 12.2 Deckbuilding
- Each deck: exactly 2 different leaders (whose faceup starting sides cannot both have Villainy AND Command aspects) + exactly 1 base + at least 80 other cards.
- No more than 1 copy of any card (including leaders, units, events, upgrades).
- Players may still control multiple copies of non-unique cards through game effects.

## 12.3 Two Leaders
- Both leaders provide their aspect icons.
- Leaders can be deployed and defeated independently. Both may be deployed simultaneously.

## 12.4 Setup
- Same as multiplayer setup (Section 11), with two adjustments:
  - a. During setup step 2: put both leaders into play below their base, Leader side faceup.
  - b. After giving the initiative counter to the first player, place the blast counter and plan counter in the center of the game area.

## 12.5 Counters
- Twin Suns requires two additional counters: **blast counter** and **plan counter.**
- Both have an additional effect when taken, resolved immediately:
  - a. **Blast counter**: deal 1 damage to each opponent's base.
  - b. **Plan counter**: draw 1 card from your deck, then place 1 card from your hand on the bottom of your deck (may place the drawn card).
- **Take an Available Counter** replaces the "Take the Initiative" action. A player takes any one available counter (initiative, blast, or plan), flips to "taken" side, and passes for the rest of the phase.
- A player can only take this action if they haven't taken a counter that round.
- A player may control more than one counter simultaneously (e.g., if they already have the initiative when they take a different counter).
- At the start of each regroup phase, return the blast and plan counters to center "available" side up.

## 12.6 Action Phase
- Available actions: Play a Card, Attack With a Unit, Use an Action Ability, or Take an Available Counter.
- Players may NOT choose Pass in place of a different action. Players may only pass if no counters are available to take, and must pass after taking a counter.
- In a 4-player game, once all 3 counters are taken, the last remaining player may pass to end the phase.

## 12.7 Ending the Game
- Once one player is eliminated, the game ends at the end of the current phase. The player with the most HP on their base wins.
- Phase-ending effects and triggers still resolve.
- If multiple players are tied for most HP, they share the victory.
- When a player eliminates another player, the eliminating player heals 5 damage from their own base. (Not applied if a player eliminates themselves.)
