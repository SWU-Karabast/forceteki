# 7.5 Keyword Abilities

> Source: Star Wars Unlimited Comprehensive Rules v7.0 (3/6/26)

---

## General Rules for Keywords (7.5.1–7.5.4)

- 7.5.1 A keyword is indicated with **bold red text** and has specific associated rules. Keywords resolve like any other ability but are not shorthand for another ability.
- 7.5.2 A keyword ability resolves automatically unless its definition includes "may."
- 7.5.3 A keyword given to a unit (via constant ability or lasting effect) is treated as though printed in the unit's text box and can be lost, ignored, or affected by other abilities.
- 7.5.4 **Stacking**: If a card is given a keyword it already has, keywords don't stack (no extra effect) unless followed by a numeral, cost, or dash+ability. If followed by a numeral, the numbers are added together. If followed by a cost or dash+text, each instance is its own independent ability.
  - *Example: Grit doesn't stack. Raid 1 + Raid 2 = Raid 3.*

---

## Ambush (7.5.5)

**Definition**: Resolves like the triggered ability: *"When Played/When Deployed/When Created: If there is an enemy unit this unit can attack, this unit may attack that enemy unit, even if this unit is exhausted."*

- a. Multiple instances don't stack — if Ambush is gained multiple times, the unit may only attack once.
- b. A unit with Ambush still enters play exhausted and cannot use Ambush if no enemy units are attackable.
- c. Ambush resolves in the same window as "When Played" abilities.
- d. An Ambush attack is resolved like any other attack with all the same steps. See 6.3.
- e. An Ambush attack resolves during the same turn the unit entered play, as a nested ability. If the active player resolves Ambush before other simultaneous triggers, those other triggers resolve after the Ambush attack (and any abilities triggered during it) finishes.

---

## Bounty (7.5.13)

**Definition**: Resolves like the triggered ability: *"When Defeated/When Captured: Resolve the following ability"* — except the triggered ability is considered controlled by an **opponent**.

- a. Each Bounty ability on a unit is independent. A unit can have multiple Bounty abilities.
- b. Bounty resolves in the same window as "When Defeated" or "When Captured" abilities.
- c. To "collect a Bounty" is to resolve a triggered Bounty ability.
- d. Resolving a Bounty is optional. Choosing not to = not considered to have collected it.
- e. Bounty is always resolved by an opponent of the unit's controller. If a player defeats/captures their own unit with Bounty, they must choose an opponent to resolve the Bounty.
- f. In multiplayer, the opponent who defeats/captures the unit is the opponent who may collect the Bounty. See 11.

---

## Coordinate (7.5.15)

**Definition**: Same as the constant ability: *"While you control 3 or more units, this card gains the following ability."*

- a. Each Coordinate ability on a unit is independent.
- b. A unit with Coordinate still has Coordinate while controlling 2 or fewer units — the ability that follows Coordinate is just inactive.

---

## Exploit X (7.5.16)

**Definition**: Same as the constant ability: *"While playing this card, you may defeat up to X friendly units. For each unit defeated this way, this card costs 2 less."*

- a. Multiple instances stack — the numerals are added together.
- b. Exploit is used during Step 3 of the "Play a Card" action (Determine Costs).
- c. "When Defeated" abilities that trigger from units defeated using Exploit resolve only after the "Play a Card" action finishes, in the same window as "When Played" abilities.

---

## Grit (7.5.6)

**Definition**: Same as the constant ability: *"This unit gets +1 power for each damage on it."*

- a. Multiple instances don't stack.
- b. When a unit with Grit deals and is dealt damage simultaneously (i.e., in combat), the unit doesn't get increased power from the new damage until after all damage is dealt.
  - *Example: A 2/2 Grit unit defending against a 1/3 attacker only deals 2 damage, even though it takes 1 combat damage.*
- c. If an ability lets a unit deal combat damage before another unit, the second unit must survive before dealing back. If the second unit has Grit, it receives bonus power from damage already on it.
  - *Example: Shoot First + Battlefield Marine (4 power) attacks Baze Malbus (Grit). Battlefield Marine deals 4 damage first. Baze gets +4/+0 from Grit and deals 6 damage back, defeating Battlefield Marine.*

---

## Hidden (7.5.18)

**Definition**: Same as the constant ability: *"This unit can't be attacked if it was played/deployed/created this phase."*

- a. If a unit has both Hidden and Sentinel, it can be attacked — abilities can't prevent Sentinel units from being attacked.

---

## Overwhelm (7.5.7)

**Definition**: Same as the constant ability: *"While attacking, this unit deals its excess combat damage to the defending player's base."*

- *Example: A unit with 5 power and Overwhelm attacks an enemy with 3 remaining HP — it defeats that unit and deals 2 damage to the base.*
- a. Multiple instances don't stack.
- b. An Overwhelm attacker deals damage to the defending unit and the enemy base simultaneously. If the defending unit wouldn't be defeated by combat damage, no damage goes to the base.
- c. When an Overwhelm attacker deals excess damage to a base, it is considered to have dealt combat damage to the base, but it is not considered to have attacked that base.
- d. If a Shield token on the defending unit prevents combat damage from an Overwhelm attacker, no damage is dealt to the base.
- e. If the defending unit leaves play before the Overwhelm attacker deals combat damage (e.g., via an "On Attack" ability), all of the attacker's combat damage is considered excess damage and dealt to the enemy base (unit is not considered attacked).

---

## Piloting [Y] (7.5.17)

**Definition**: Same as the constant ability: *"You may play this unit as an upgrade on a friendly VEHICLE unit without a PILOT upgrade on it by paying cost Y instead of its printed cost."*

- a. Each Piloting ability on a unit is independent.
- b. A card with Piloting is considered an upgrade for abilities that say "play an upgrade" or "play a non-unit card." If an ability says "play a unit," the unit can only be played as a unit (not an upgrade via Piloting). If "play a card," can be played as either.
- c. Units with Piloting are still considered units in out-of-play zones. Abilities that look for upgrades in out-of-play zones don't find Piloting units (unless the ability says "play an upgrade").

---

## Plot (7.5.19)

**Definition**: Resolves like the triggered ability: *"When you deploy a leader: You may play this card from your resource zone by paying its cost, replacing it with the top card of your deck. This ability is active while this card is a resource."*

- a. You may play any number of Plot cards when you deploy a leader. Show them all to your opponent, then play them one at a time in any order. Resolve triggered abilities from each before playing the next.
- b. As it enters play, a Plot card is replaced in the resource zone by the top card of the deck (the two cards enter play simultaneously; the new resource enters play exhausted).
- c. A card with Plot must already be in the resource zone when the leader is deployed. Replacement cards from the deck's top that happen to have Plot can't be played as part of the same deploy action.

---

## Raid X (7.5.8)

**Definition**: Same as the constant ability: *"While attacking, this unit gets +X power,"* where X is the number following Raid.

- a. Multiple instances **stack** — the numerals are added together.
  - *Example: Raid 1 + Raid 2 = Raid 3 → +3 power while attacking.*
- b. The attacker gets bonus power for the duration of the attack (same as "While this unit is attacking") and loses it when the attack ends.
- c. If a unit gains or loses Raid during an attack, its power is immediately adjusted.

---

## Restore X (7.5.9)

**Definition**: Resolves like the triggered ability: *"On Attack: Heal X damage from your base,"* where X is the number following Restore.

- a. Multiple instances **stack** — the numerals are added together.
  - *Example: Restore 1 + Restore 2 = Restore 3 → heals 3 damage when attacking.*

---

## Saboteur (7.5.10)

**Definition**: Encompasses two abilities:
1. Constant ability: *"You may ignore Sentinel when choosing what this unit attacks."*
2. Triggered ability: *"On Attack: Defeat all Shield tokens attached to the defending unit."*

- a. To "ignore" Sentinel = determine the unit/base to attack as if no enemy units in the attacker's arena have Sentinel. Enemy units do NOT lose Sentinel while it's ignored.

---

## Sentinel (7.5.11)

**Definition**: Same as the constant ability: *"Units in this arena can't attack your non-Sentinel units or your base. Abilities this unit has or gains can't prevent this unit from being attacked."*

- a. After exhausting the attacker, check if the defending player has any units with Sentinel in the attacker's arena. If yes, one of those must be the defending unit — unless the attacker has Saboteur.
- b. If multiple enemy units have Sentinel in the same arena, the attacking player may choose any one of them.
- c. If the attacker has Saboteur, it may ignore Sentinel and attack any unit in the same arena or the opponent's base.
- d. If a unit has Sentinel AND a "can't be attacked" ability, Sentinel overrides — that unit CAN be attacked.

---

## Shielded (7.5.12)

**Definition**: Resolves like the triggered ability: *"When Played/When Deployed/When Created: Give a Shield token to this unit."*

- a. Shielded resolves in the same window as "When Played" abilities.
- b. Shield tokens: See 3.7.6 for Shield token rules. Key rule: damage to the attached unit is prevented; if it is, defeat one Shield token on it. Only one Shield per damage instance.

---

## Smuggle [Y] (7.5.14)

**Definition**: Same as the constant ability: *"You may play this card from your resource zone by paying cost Y instead of its printed cost, replacing it with the top card of your deck. This ability is active while this card is a resource."*

- a. Each Smuggle ability is independent. A unit can have multiple Smuggle abilities.
- b. Playing via Smuggle is a modified "Play a Card" action, overriding the restriction that cards must be played from hand.
- c. While in the resource zone, a facedown card with Smuggle on its facedown side is considered to have Smuggle. Opponents can't see the Smuggle ability (hidden information).
- d. A Smuggle card can exhaust itself to help pay its own Smuggle cost (it's still a resource while paying costs).
- e. A unit/upgrade played via Smuggle enters play and is considered "played." "When Played" abilities trigger. The unit enters exhausted.
- f. The card is replaced in the resource zone by the top card of the deck simultaneously as it enters play. The replacement resource enters exhausted.
- g. A player may use Smuggle with an empty deck. They don't replace the card, don't draw, and don't deal damage to their base for a failed draw.
- h. Abilities active "while playing the card" become active as usual during Step 1 of the "Play a Card" action.
